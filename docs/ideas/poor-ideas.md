# Poor Ideas (1/3 — parked, not adopted)

From the [roundtable](roundtable.md). Each got exactly one KEEP vote. Not dead forever — parked with the
reason it fell short, in case a future revision rescues the intent.

- **S5 · Throughput Gates** *(kept by Mira only)* — deepest skill nodes require a minimum sustained ◈/s
  income, not just a stockpile.
  *Why it fell: Vex & Rook both flagged it as anti-cozy — a hidden wall that punishes the idler who
  stockpiled overnight. The good intent (income matters more than hoarding) already lives in the accepted
  **S3 Mastery Overflow**.*

- **S9 · Opposition Meter** *(kept by Mira only)* — a balance-wheel of the six essences that flags when
  lopsided investment throttles an opposite.
  *Why it fell: redundant — the accepted **S2 Opposition Tax** already surfaces that tension in the tree
  math. Could return later as S2's small visual "face" if S2 needs one, but not as its own idea.*

- **S11 · Constellations** *(kept by Vex only)* — render the skill DAG as star-maps; completing a closed
  loop of nodes lights a themed "Constellation" passive.
  *Why it fell: gorgeous but a heavy build on a static text DAG, and Mira argued the star-map skin obscures
  the actual prerequisites (legibility cost). Possible far-future cosmetic flourish, not a core system.*

- **S12 · Musing / Insight half-life** *(kept by Rook only)* — a small Insight pool slowly decays into
  permanent progress on whichever branch you're currently viewing.
  *Why it fell: genuinely novel ("a mage lost in thought"), but Vex & Mira both called it anti-idle — a soft
  loss mechanic that rewards tab-fiddling and punishes stepping away, against the core pillar.*

- **C12 · Overchannel** *(kept by Rook only)* — hold/toggle a cast to spend double Mana for a chance at a
  critical "surge," at the cost of a small backlash to a random essence.
  *Why it fell: a fun signature verb for active hunts, but two designers saw the random-essence backlash as
  an anti-cozy gamble; the accepted **C3 Stability vs. Overload** covers casting risk more cleanly (and, per
  the referee note there, without resource "theft").*

# Round 2 (Fable) — 1/3 parked

- **R2-M2 · Overflow Channels** *(kept by Vex only — his own)* — capped essence overflow routes to a
  wheel-adjacent element at a lossy rate, or trickles to Prismatic.
  *Why it fell: Mira and Rook both read it as round 1's rejected Byproduct Pipelines "with the decay filed
  off" — lossy hidden plumbing, illegible. The live intent ("caps shouldn't waste overnight") could return
  as a single simple toggle, not a routing system.*

- **R2-S1 · Confluence Nodes** *(kept by Vex only — his own)* — unbuyable nodes whose level is computed
  live as the geometric mean of two feeder skills.
  *Why it fell: "a spreadsheet formula in a robe" (Rook); violates numbers-always-sourced legibility (Mira);
  and round 1's accepted Cross-Branch Hybrids already owns "two branches make a third thing."*

- **R2-S3 · Curriculum Endowment** *(kept by Rook only — ironically not its author Vex)* — flag owned nodes
  as Academy curriculum for an Insight tithe + prestige re-learning discount.
  *Why it fell: the teaching cluster collapsed to one system — **R2-S9 Teacher Learns Twice** won the vote
  and absorbs this idea's kernel (visible pips, no tithe bookkeeping; the prestige-discount half double-dips
  R2-S2 Palimpsest).*

- **R2-C4 · Thesis Defense** *(kept by Rook only — again not its author Vex)* — appoint one spell as your
  teaching thesis; students grind its Mastery.
  *Why it fell: same teaching-cluster collapse — **R2-C12 The School Spell** won and absorbs the per-spell
  Mastery kernel. One "students practice my spell" system, not two.*

# Round 3 (Longevity Council) — 1/3 parked

- **K4 · Envoys to the Hidden Courts** *(kept by Marrow only — his own)* — faction trade caravans with
  posted rates, moods, and Embassies (KG trading).
  *Why it fell: duplicates the Contract board's "outside world wants things" fantasy at triple the UI cost,
  and market "moods" edge toward timing pressure. Revisit only if a dedicated Trade layer is ever wanted.*

- **K7 · Bloodline Grafts** *(kept by Marrow only — his own)* — CRISPR-style Aeon tree to remove Lineage
  flaws and compose custom Lineages (Evolve CRISPR).
  *Why it fell: sanding the flaws off K6's Lineages "quietly murders the variety engine" (Tempo), and it's
  a second permanent meta-tree before the first exists (Atlas). Revisit after K6 ships if players ask.*

- **K9 · Rites of Divergent Ascension** *(kept by Marrow only — his own)* — alternate reset verbs, each
  with its own stacking currency and shop (Evolve prestige ladder).
  *Why it fell: three verbs × three currencies × three shops = currency sprawl on a game with five meta
  layers already. The goal-shape variety it wanted lives more cheaply in Vows (K8) + Sites (K16).*

- **K14 · Unspoken Vows** *(kept by Tempo only — his own)* — the game silently notices emergent abstentions
  and names the vow retroactively (KG Iron Will).
  *Why it fell: "an economy tracked in invisible ink" (Marrow); and accepted **R2-S11 Witnessed Lore**
  already owns "the game noticed what you lived," while **K8** owns vows — two idioms for one idea.*

- **K18 · Run Charters** *(kept by Tempo only — his own)* — Sprint/Season/Saga run-scoping with tuned
  payout curves (Evolve run lengths).
  *Why it fell: invisible payout-curve math + a third run-start ceremony (after Lineage and Site drafts).
  Its wisdom — teach run-length rhythm in-game — folds into **K13 The Astronomer's Counsel**.*
