# Design Roundtable — Round 3: The Longevity Council (Kittens Game × Evolve)

A three-specialist council (on Fable) studied two acclaimed, long-lived idlers — **Kittens Game**
(wiki.kittensgame.com) and **Evolve** (pmotschmann) — to mine what makes them good and *long-lasting*,
then adapted those elements to our design (including a direct review of the tab set). Same rules:
3/3 consensus & 2/3 majority → add · 1/3 → poor · 0/3 → reject.

## The council
- **Marrow, the Economist** — resource webs, caps/storage, price ratios, layered prestige currencies.
- **Tempo, the Pacing Director** — the unfold, walls, session rhythm, run-length design, reset timing.
- **Atlas, the Interface Cartographer** — tab systems, progressive disclosure, queues, tooltips, settings.

## What the source games taught (as distilled by the council)
- **Kittens Game:** per-resource **caps** drive all early decisions; **price-ratio** meta-buys ("make every
  future purchase cheaper") are the most exciting purchase in the genre; the **unfold** (tabs appearing as
  eras) is the greatest onboarding trick; deliberate **walls** are the curriculum; a **parallel vertical
  chain** (unicorns) is a second game to rotate to; **dry flavor prose** is what players quote a decade later.
- **Evolve:** runs must **diverge at minute one** (species drafts with real flaws); the **build queue** is
  the session tool that turns 2-minute check-ins into 40 minutes of intent; **terrain drafts** (planets)
  re-price a whole run with zero new content; **challenge genes** let veterans manufacture their own
  difficulty for meta pay; an **in-game wiki** (every noun explainable) is how 300 mechanics stay navigable;
  **settings depth** (notation, themes, density) lets spreadsheet and phone players share one game.

## The fights worth recording
- **Declared vs. silent vows:** Marrow's opt-in **Sworn Vows (K8)** beat Tempo's retroactive **Unspoken
  Vows (K14)** 2–1 — "an economy tracked in invisible ink is a slot machine" (Marrow); Atlas added that
  accepted *Witnessed Lore* already owns "the game noticed what you lived."
- **Prestige-scoping stalemate:** Marrow's **Rites (K9)** and Tempo's **Charters (K18)** each kept their own
  and cut the other's; Atlas cut *both* as currency/ceremony sprawl → both parked at 1/3.
- **The seasons variant flip:** Marrow and Tempo *swapped* positions on K5 (player-chosen vs rotating
  calendar); Atlas + Marrow settled it **player-chosen** (a calendar that rotates without you edges FOMO).
- **Queue convergence:** all three independently demanded ONE queue idiom (K10+K17 merged, two lanes).
- **Self-cuts:** Marrow cut his own purchase queue; Tempo cut his own First Spark; Atlas cut his own `*`
  marker as "one column of pixels, one ballot line."

## Resulting tab-set direction (from K11 + K20 + K21 + K22 — consensus)
The rail slims from 9 to **7 tabs**, appearing one by one KG-style:
`Main · Skills · Home · Spell Craft · Satchel (Gear/Potions) · Bestiary · Academy(greyed beacon → sub-tabs: Halls · Students · Faculty · Contracts · Ascension)` — Player folds into the right-hand
Character panel (click to expand); Codex & System stay in the header. *(Not yet applied to doc 04 / the
mockup — pending the integration pass.)*
