# Design Roundtable — Main screen · Skills · Spell Craft

A three-designer idea contest. Three agents generated ideas in character, then debated and voted
KEEP/CUT on the pooled 43-idea ballot. I refereed the tally by the agreed rule:

- **3/3 → add (consensus)** · **2/3 → add (majority)** → [accepted.md](accepted.md)
- **1/3 → [poor-ideas.md](poor-ideas.md)** · **0/3 → [rejected.md](rejected.md)**

## The designers (biases)
- **Vex — the Depth Architect.** Interlocking systems, meaningful numbers, emergent optimization. Watches: over-complication.
- **Mira — the Player's Advocate.** Clarity, onboarding, feel, legibility, cozy pacing. Watches: shallowness.
- **Rook — the Wildcard.** Novelty, identity, bold hooks. Watches: gimmicks & scope creep.

## What the debate revealed
- **Strong cross-bias consensus on legibility** — the "no mystery math" UX cluster (payoff previews, sourced node effects, auto-pause explained, cast affordability, At-N chips) swept 3/3. Even Vex called these "the pillar made real."
- **The cozy/idle-first pillar was the main killer.** Every idea with a *timed FOMO* or *decay/punish* edge died or nearly died: Conjunctions (0), Musing half-life (1), Throughput Gates (1), Byproduct decay (0), Standing Attunement drain (0), Overchannel backlash (1). Mira and Vex both invoked "anti-idle."
- **Drag-and-drop spectacle lost to the text/number frame.** Two Hands, Rituals, and the two-page Grimoire drag-layout were cut or self-cut (Rook policed his own scope).
- **Designers cut their own darlings.** Vex axed Byproduct Pipelines & Contract Ledger; Rook axed 4 of his 5 Main ideas; Mira axed her own Opposition Meter's kin. Good faith.
- **Convergence = signal.** The two merged ideas (M4 Momentum, S2 Opposition Tax) both landed 3/3 — independent invention by two designers predicted consensus.

`✔`=KEEP, `✗`=CUT. Verdict → file.
