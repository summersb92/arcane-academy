# Design Roundtable — Round 2 (Fable) + UI Panel

Round 2 re-ran the contest with the same three personas on **Claude Fable** (stronger model), briefed on
round 1's verdicts and required to go *beyond* them. A separate **two-designer UI panel** (on Opus) audited
the actual mockup. Rules: trio → 3/3 consensus & 2/3 majority add, 1/3 poor, 0/3 reject. UI pair → 2/2 add,
split = referee decides, 0/2 reject.

## The designers
- **Vex** (depth), **Mira** (UX/clarity), **Rook** (novelty/identity) — same personas as round 1, Fable-powered.
- **Serif — the Information Designer** (typography/hierarchy/accessibility purist; audited the mockup's real CSS).
- **Flux — the Feel Engineer** (micro-feedback, motion-with-restraint, mobile ergonomics).

## What round 2 revealed
- **Fable went for composition.** Nearly every idea wires *two existing systems together* (Bestiary→Skills,
  Spell Craft→Home, Academy→your task cards, Roster→At-N). Round 1 filled screens; round 2 built plumbing.
- **The "teaching cluster" self-policed.** Five overlapping "students grind my stuff while I idle" ideas
  (S3/S9/C4/C12/M4) — each designer cut their *own* duplicate in favor of a rival's stronger twin. The
  survivors (S9, C12, M4) carry explicit merge notes.
- **Round 1's law held.** The two casualties echo round 1's kills: Overflow Channels died as "Byproduct
  Pipelines with the decay filed off," Confluence Nodes as illegible math ("a spreadsheet formula in a robe").
- **UI panel near-consensus.** Serif and Flux agreed on 15/19 instantly — including each cutting one of
  their own (Serif's zone-tinting "decoration," Flux's double-dipped completion pulse).
