# 09 — Magic, Vitals & the Personal Systems

This doc defines the **Arcanum-style solo-mage core**: the game is played *as a character* first, and the
Academy (doc 01) is one system that character eventually builds. It supersedes the old "five Spellcaster
schools / five manas" model.

## Layout contract (the frame)
- **LEFT column — Resources (physical):** Gold, Insight, Renown, crafting **Materials** (reagents, ore,
  gems, monster parts), and meta currencies (Aeon, Legacy). The ledger of *things you have*.
- **TOP — Tabs:** `Main · Skills · Player · Home · Spell Craft · Equipment · Potions · Bestiary · Academy`.
- **RIGHT column — Character (player properties):** your **Vitals** and **Essence**, always visible:
  - **Vitals:** ✚ Life · ⚡ Stamina · ✦ Mana (each current / max, with regen).
  - **Essence:** ❖ Prismatic · ▲ Fire · ▼ Water · ⬢ Earth · ≈ Air · ☾ Dark · ☀ Light (amount + rate).

## 1. The elements
Six base elements in **three opposed pairs**, plus **Prismatic** (the unified master element):

| Element | Glyph | Opposes | Flavor / niche |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fire** | ▲ | Water | aggression, damage, forging, haste |
| **Water** | ▼ | Fire | flow, healing-over-time, cold, control |
| **Earth** | ⬢ | Air | defense, materials, endurance, wealth |
| **Air** | ≈ | Earth | speed, insight, evasion, lightning |
| **Light** | ☀ | Dark | healing, wards, order, revealing |
| **Dark** | ☾ | Light | necromancy, lifesteal, secrets, high yield |
| **Prismatic** | ❖ | — | all elements at once; the endgame; hardest to make |

**Opposition rules (tunable):**
- **Combat:** a creature/spell of element X takes extra damage from its *opposite* and resists its own
  element (Bestiary §7). Choosing the right element is the core combat decision.
- **Generation tension:** heavily favoring one element gives a small **penalty to its opposite's** essence
  rate — so lopsided builds are strong but brittle; balanced builds unlock Prismatic.
- **Prismatic** essence only accrues while you hold **meaningful amounts of all six**; its spells/effects are
  the most powerful and are a natural endgame / prestige target.

Essence is generated by: your **Home** fixtures, **Skills**, **equipment/affinity**, **Bestiary** drops,
and — after the Founding — the **Academy's** element halls. Essence is *spent* to inscribe spells (Spell
Craft), brew tonics, and power typed effects.

## 2. Vitals
| Vital | Glyph | Regen | Spent on | If depleted |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Life** | ✚ | slow passive + potions/Light | taking hits in Bestiary hunts & risky contracts | **Downed:** you retreat, lose the hunt & some essence/materials, respawn at Home. Never a hard game-over. |
| **Stamina** | ⚡ | steady passive | active abilities, labor (Act I), exploring, hunts | actions queue/block until it recovers |
| **Mana** | ✦ | passive + Water/Focus | **casting spells** (the general pool) | can't cast until it recovers |

Vitals have a **max** raised by level, attributes, equipment, and Skills. This is the layer that makes the
Bestiary matter without any base-defense/Blight mechanic.

## 3. Player (character sheet)
- **Level & XP** — from hunts, contracts, research, graduations. Level raises vital maxes & unlocks skills.
- **Attributes** (3): **Might** (Life, melee/physical, carry), **Focus** (Mana, cast power, crafting),
  **Attunement** (essence rates, elemental potency, Prismatic access). Points allotted on level-up.
- **Resistances** — per element (reduce incoming typed damage); shaped by equipment & Skills.
- **Titles** — flavor milestones (Stable-hand → Apprentice → Adept → … → Archmage), some with small perks.

## 4. Skills (the progression web — formerly "Research")
A branching DAG spent with **Insight** (◈). It's the same engine doc 05 called `research`, renamed and
front-and-centre. Branches:
- **Elementalism** (one sub-branch per element + a Prismatic capstone) — essence rates, spell tiers, potency.
- **Focus** — Mana pool/regen, cast cost reduction, concentration.
- **Scribing** — Spell Craft options (new shapes/components), scroll crafting.
- **Alchemy** — Potions recipes & potency, material yields.
- **Combat** — Life, resistances, hunt success, dispatch power.
- **Scholarship** — Insight rate, cheaper skills, and the **Academy** unlocks (teaching, capacity, futures).

The first few nodes are literally your Act I cantrips (doc 08); the web then fans out across all systems.

## 5. Home (personal base → academy seed)
Your dwelling, furnished with **fixtures** that give passive bonuses:
- *Study Desk* → Insight · *Alembic* → potion brewing · *Hearth* → Fire essence · *Ossuary* → Dark essence ·
  *Library* → Skill discounts · *Ward Stones* → resistances, etc.
- Home has a soft capacity (upgradeable). It is the **precursor to the Academy**: at the **Founding**
  (doc 08) your Home becomes the academy's first hall and fixtures seed the first rooms.
- Playing **The Recluse** challenge (doc 03) means growing the Home *instead of* an Academy the whole run.

## 6. Spell Craft
Compose spells from three parts, then **inscribe** them (costs essence) to add to your book:
- **Element** (Fire…Dark, or Prismatic) — sets damage/effect type & essence cost.
- **Shape** (Bolt, Nova, Aura, Ward, Beam…) — targeting/behavior.
- **Component** (Siphon = lifesteal, Rend = armor break, Chill = slow, Echo = repeat…) — a modifier.
- Casting an inscribed spell spends **Mana** (✦); its power scales with Attunement, skills, affinity, gear.
- **Prismatic** spells require balanced essence and are the strongest (endgame). Example composition:
  *Dark ☾ + Bolt + Siphon → "Umbral Bolt (Siphon)"* — Dark damage that heals you.

## 7. Bestiary (light personal combat & gathering)
A catalogue of creatures, each with an **element affinity**, **threat** tier, and **drop table** (essence +
materials). This is where **Life** is at stake and where opposition matters.
- **Hunt** a creature: an idle/active encounter resolved by your spells, gear, Life, and element choice
  (bring the creature's *opposite* element). Success → essence + materials + XP; failure → get Downed (§2).
- **Study** to unlock lore, better drops, and eventually **summon/tame** (turn a foe into a helper or, at the
  Academy, a teaching beast — a nod to Spellcaster's Nature beasts).
- Creatures scale with your Era/prestige; the **Prism Wyrm** (all-element) is a rare, powerful capstone hunt.
- This replaces Arcanum's dungeons and gives combat Futures (doc 07) real prey to be good at.

## 8. Equipment
Slots: **Focus** (wand/staff — cast power), **Robes** (defense/resist), **Ring**, **Amulet**. Items grant
flat/percent bonuses, element **affinity** (+that element's potency/rate), and resistances. Sourced from
crafting (Materials + essence), Bestiary drops, contracts, and events. A simple inventory holds the rest.

## 9. Potions
Brew consumables at the Home **Alembic** from **Materials** (+ time, + a little essence):
- Restoratives (Life/Stamina/Mana), essence tonics (bank a burst of one element), combat buffs, and utility
  (e.g., "elixir of balance" nudging toward Prismatic). Held potions are usable from any tab — your panic button in a hunt.

## 10. How this maps onto the Academy (doc 01)
When you found the Academy, the same elements carry over: **halls teach elements**, **students have element
affinities & traits**, they graduate into **Futures**, and you **dispatch** them on contracts/hunts. The
solo systems don't switch off — you keep playing your character *and* run the institution, with the Academy
massively scaling your essence/gold/insight. (Element renames from the old model: Arcane→folded into
Air/Prismatic, Nature→Earth, Shadow→Dark, Alchemy→the Potions system + Fire/Water crafting.)
